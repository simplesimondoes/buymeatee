# 11. Risks and Technical Debt

## Product risks

- **Chicken-and-egg creator acquisition** — supporters need creators, creators need supporters. Early-access validation targets both sides for this reason.
- **Unvalidated demand** — "buy a tee" may not resonate; validation questions in [.ai/context/product.md](../.ai/context/product.md) exist to test this before building the app.

## Future platform risks (must inform, not block, current work)

- **Payment and payout complexity** — marketplace-style money movement, fees, VAT, cross-border.
- **Identity verification** — KYC for creators receiving money.
- **Refunds and chargebacks** — supporter disputes against goal-based contributions.
- **Creator moderation** — fake journeys, impersonation, inappropriate content.
- **Misuse of funds** — money raised for a goal spent elsewhere; expectation-setting needed.
- **Minor creators** — parent/guardian requirements, platform liability, stricter rules.
- **Country-specific regulation** — payment services, consumer protection and (for amateurs) sponsorship/amateur-status rules vary by jurisdiction.
- **Platform fees** — unannounced; must be published transparently before launch.
- **Copyright and image licensing** — golf course and creator imagery must be licensed or original.

## Current-phase risks and known debt

- **Spam submissions** — the early-access form will attract bots; boundary needs honeypot/rate limiting when built.
- **Temporary legal copy** — privacy and terms are drafts marked for legal review; launching without real legal review is a risk to track.
- **Placeholder photography** — launch imagery must be licensed/original; placeholders tracked in the image requirements document (to be created in Wave 1).
- **Pre-code documentation debt** — several skills and arc42 chapters describe *planned* structure; they must be updated to reality as Wave 1 lands, or they become misleading.
