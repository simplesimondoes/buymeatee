# Product context — BuyMeATee

## Vision

BuyMeATee is where golf fans help creators play more, achieve more and chase their goals. It is a golf-native creator-support platform built around real golfing goals, visible progress and an ongoing connection between creators and supporters.

It must never feel like a generic tipping site with golf styling, a charity, or a crowdfunding campaign. The emotional proposition is **Support the journey** — supporters are invited to follow and participate in a real golfing journey, not asked for money.

## Audiences

**Creators:** YouTube/TikTok/Instagram golf creators, amateur tournament golfers, aspiring professionals, golf coaches, course reviewers, golf travel creators, women's golf creators, adaptive golf creators, junior golfers (represented by a parent or guardian). The platform should feel appropriate for small and emerging creators, not only famous influencers.

**Supporters:** golf fans who follow these creators and want a more meaningful way to back them than a "coffee".

## Jobs to be done

- Creator: "Help me fund the golf I need to play (green fees, travel, entries, equipment, coaching, production) so I can keep creating and competing."
- Creator: "Turn my followers into participants in my journey."
- Supporter: "Let me back a golfer I believe in, towards something specific, and see the progress I helped make possible."

## Value propositions

- **Goal based** — support goes towards visible, real golfing goals with progress.
- **Golf native** — tees, holes, rounds and green fees as the support mechanic (1 Tee, 3 Tees, 9 Holes, 18 Holes, Green Fee, Custom).
- **Community first** — updates, milestones and connection, not a one-off transaction.
- **More than a tip jar** — a journey creates a story; a coffee is quickly forgotten.

## Core terminology

Creator, Supporter, Goal, Journey, Buy a tee, Support a round, Green fee, Early access. Never: donation, recipient, begging, crowdfunding campaign. See [brand.md](brand.md) and [arc42/12-glossary.md](../../arc42/12-glossary.md).

## Current hypothesis

Golf creators and their audiences want a golf-native, goal-based support platform, and enough of both sides will register early-access interest to justify building the full product.

## Validation questions

- Will golf creators share a BuyMeATee page?
- Will supporters understand "buy a tee"?
- Do supporters prefer general support or specific goals?
- Which creator segments are most interested?
- What support amounts feel natural?
- Do supporters want updates, badges or exclusive content?
- How much trust information is needed before contributing?
