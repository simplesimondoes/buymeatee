# Skill: Content

## Terminology and voice

Full brand reference: [.ai/context/brand.md](../context/brand.md). In short: BuyMeATee (exact spelling), Creator/Supporter/Goal/Journey vocabulary, warm aspirational golf-literate tone, **British English** throughout.

## Labelling fiction

Every fictional creator, goal, progress figure or UI preview is labelled `Example`, `Preview` or `Concept` — visibly, near the content, not in a distant footnote. Fictional people must not be presentable as real customers.

## Claims that must not be made

- No live payments, fees, provider protection, launch dates.
- No user counts, reviews, testimonials, partners, press or funding.
- No team, headquarters or launch history on About.
- Payment wording: the product is in early development; final payment and fee details will be published transparently before launch.
- Amateur-status content: never give definitive rulings — state that governing-body, competition and jurisdiction rules vary and must be checked.
- Minors: accounts involving minors will require an appropriate parent or guardian; never imply minors contract independently.

## Blog conventions

> **Status: planned** — update with the real content location and format once the blog architecture exists (expected: local MDX or typed structured content under the repo, no CMS).

- Author label: `BuyMeATee Editorial` (honest, no invented bylines).
- Each article: title, slug, description, author, publication date, updated date, reading time, hero image, body, internal links, Article structured data.
- 900–1,500 words where appropriate; genuinely useful; no AI filler, keyword stuffing or unsupported statistics.
- Adding an article = add the content file + hero image slot + sitemap entry (automatic if the sitemap is dynamic) + internal links from related pages.

## Review expectations

All new or changed copy runs the [content checklist](../quality-gates/content-checklist.md). Copy affecting claims, privacy or payments also gets a [security](../agents/security.md) read.
